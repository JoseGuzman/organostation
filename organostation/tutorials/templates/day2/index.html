1. Wide-field fluorescence

The standard system contains a long-range macroscopic (0.6- 1.7X) magnification that includes epifluorescence illumination with a large (~ 4 cm) working distance and a photo/video capture of an area of 12 x 9 mm. This configuration may change depending on your specific needs.

The imaging will be for a Green-fluorescence protein (GFP, see image below) or a Red-fluorescence protein (RFP) acquisition, depending on your ordering. 

  • Filter sets for GFP-imaging: 470/40 nm excitation filter, Longpass 490 nm Dichroic Mirror, and a 550/50 nm emission filter.
  • Filter sets for RFP-imaging: 590 nm emission

Adjustments must be made to climate the light beam on the specimen. It is essential to set the dichroic filter 45 degrees from the incidence light and adjust the collimator to obtain the maximal intensity at the sample. The microscope must be located at about  4 cm, which permits inserting the samples and the probes comfortably.

1.1 Image acquisition 

The video camera requires a fast (USB 3.0) connection to the computer and records at a maximum of 59 frames per second. Adjustments should be made

 

1.2 Video Acquisition

It is recommended to use different security sockets for the recording system and the computer. If possible, two independent extra-long power strips are advisable

2. Electronic components

Connect the power supply and USB cable of the rear part from the open-ephys board. The front part contains the SPI cables that will go directly to the 64-channel headstage. The location of that stage is essential for mapping the electrodes in the right position. The headstage is connected to the neural probe through an omnetics connector.

2.1 Documenting the probes

  • Annotate the serial number and type of the probe:
  • The accompanying documentation must include the mapping of the electrodes
  • Keep those two documents for your dataset. They are important to monitor your experiments later
  • Remove both grounding and reference electrodes from your probes.

3. Mechanical components

3.1 Micromanipulators

First, adjust the location of the LN to the locations on the table, and adjust them according to the purple line in the figures. We recommend an angle of incidence of 30 degrees.

Micromanipulators must be connected to LN-control box SM10 following the color code (z-direction in red, y-direction in green, and x-direction in yellow).

Connect the LAN port of the Remote control SM10 with the LAN port of the Control box SM10.

It is important to keep both Control Box and remote control OFF

4. Drug application system

Precise temperature control for solution and organoid is necessary to preserve physiological conditions.

The pump must be connected to the temperature controller and the chamber.
