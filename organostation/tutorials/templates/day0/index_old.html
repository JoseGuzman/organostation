1. Hardware requirements

Please, ask your building facility to assist you when installing the system for the first time. The equipment is heavy, but it is possible to move it with the help of a hand pallet trolley.

1. 1 Dedicated location

Depending on your model, you will have a 600 x 600 cm (Width x Length) for the recording system, or a 600 x 900 cm table passive isolator table for the recording workstation (see figure). Note that the table requires a surrounding distance of ~ 20 cm for electrical and mechanical isolation.

A pump will be used to pressurize the passive vibration table. We recommend filling the tables with 60 psi (415 kPa or 4,15 bars).

1. 2 Power supply

It is recommended to use different security sockets for the recording system and the computer. If possible, two independent extra long power strips are advisable.

2. Maintenance

2.1 Carbogen bottle

A mixture of 5% CO2 and 95 % O2 (carbogen, available for example, here) must be continuously supplied to the tissue to maintaining the pH and appropriate oxygenation levels. Commercially available bottles containing 50L or 10 L of carbogen at 200 bars are available.

2. 2 Pressure adaptor

If your facility does not provide it, you will need an adaptor to reduce the pressure of the carbogen bottle (200 Bar) to pressure of application necessary to operate the recording system (to ~ 1-2 bar pressure). Please, also follow the local regulation (in Europe we follow ISO 5171 regulation). Be sure that:

  1. The adaptor is suitable for conducting flammable gas.
  1. Input pressure is 200 bar (in Europe DIN-477-1).
  1. Output pressure is in the range of 1-2 bars.
  1. Output size of the adaptor (possibly diameter of 6 or 8 mm).

2. 3 Filter-candle tubing

Carbogen must be applied to the tissue through standard silicon cables via micro-filter candles. (e.g. models 18114 or 18124 in Robusglass, see this link). Be sure you have several meters of ROTILABO silicon tubes of different diameters (available for example here). Finally, you may also need tubing clamps to regulate the gas influx.

3. 1 Agarose 4% (w/v) in PBS

Prepare 4% (m/v) low-melt agarose (Biozime, Cat. No. 850080) in Phosphate Buffer Saline (PBS). We can store them in 1-2 mL stocks in Eppendorf tubes of 1.5 to 2 mL before warming them at ~ 40 degrees and embedding the tissue. If you do not have commercially available PBS, use the recipe below.
