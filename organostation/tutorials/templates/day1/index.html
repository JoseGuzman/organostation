Before we start

This guide contains a list of requirements before using our customized recording system (e.g., organostation).

In addition, before starting the recordings (see in Sample preparation), we need to be sure that we can Maintain physiological conditions, and also have prepared Solutions and drugs. Feel free to download this guide and ensure ALL elements in the Checklist are available before we arrive at your destination.

Checklist

  • 3- to 6-month-old organoids cultured in BrainPhys
  • Dedicated location for equipment (avoid direct electrical or thermal irradiation)
  • Two dedicated power sockets or outlet power strips
  • Carbogen bottle (10 L or 50 L)
  • Pressure adaptor (from 200 Bar to 1-2 Bar)
  • Filter-candle tubes
  • Silicon tubes and plastic clamps
  • 1-2 mL stocks of 4% agarose in Phosphate buffer saline (PBS)
  • 1000-500 mL of freshly prepared (i.e., same-day) artificial cerebrospinal fluid (ACSF)
  • 0.1 mL frozen aliquots containing drugs of interest
  • Python ≥ 3.8 and Matlab (version > R2016b) with toolboxes (optional)

1. Hardware requirements

The equipment purchased is relatively large (~ 1 m3), and heavy (~ 100 Kg). It permits you to perform stable recordings while keeping the user comfortable and isolated from mechanical and electrical artifacts. The isolation is assured whenever minimal laboratory conditions are guaranteed. The equipment should be preferentially located on the ground floor, away from elevators, mechanical stairs, or scientific instrumentation generating big vibrations (like microcentrifuge, rotavapors, etc.). The building facility will assist you when installing the system for the first time. Although the equipment is heavy, it is possible to move it with the help of a hand pallet trolley.

1.1 Dedicated location

You may need a quiet place for your station with the proper size. Depending on your model, ensure you have a 600 x 600 cm (width x length) for the recording system or a 600 x 900 cm passive isolator table for the recording workstation (see figure). Note that the table requires a surrounding distance of ~ 20 cm for electrical and mechanical isolation.

Computers, cell phones, or any other radio-emitting devices should not be exposed to the entry of the Faraday Cage, as they may interfere with the signals.

A pump will be used to pressurize the passive vibration table. We recommend filling the tables with 60 psi (415 kPa or 4.15 bars).

1.2 Power supply

It is recommended to use at least two different security sockets. One is for the acquisition system, which also includes the computer. The second one is for mechanical micromanipulation and the microscope. If possible, a third socket for drug application is advisable, although not entirely necessary.  

2. Maintenance of physiological conditions

Recording under physiological conditions requires detailed control of temperature and pressure, and also precise concentrations of solutions and gases. We will prepare a solution that mimics the cerebrospinal fluids (artificial cerebrospinal fluid, ACSF) that contains a carbonate gas that permits control of the pH around physiological levels. Although pH control can be achieved with chemical buffers (e.g., HEPES) or other organic acids (e.g., ascorbic acid), the CO3/CO2 buffers permit perfect equilibration and rapid control of pH.

2.1 Carbogen bottle

A mixture of 5% CO2 and 95 % O2 (carbogen, available, for example, here) applied continuously to the ACSF solution will maintain pH at ~ 7.30 and maintain the appropriate oxygenation levels. Commercially available bottles containing 50L or 10 L of carbogen at 200 bars are available.

Carbogen is highly flammable. Check the safety regulations of your institution carefully.

2.2 Pressure adaptor

If your facility does not provide it, you will need an adaptor to reduce the pressure of the carbogen bottle (200 Bar) to the pressure of application necessary to operate the recording system (to ~ 1-2 bar pressure, 1 bar is 14.5 psi or 100 kPa). Please, also follow the local regulation (in Europe, we follow ISO 5171 regulations). Be sure that:

  1. The adaptor is suitable for conducting flammable gas.
  2. Input pressure is 200 bar ( in Europe DIN-477-1).
  3. Output pressure is in the range of 1-2 bars.
  4. The output size of the adaptor has a diameter of 6 or 8 mm.

You can order a suitable adaptor here that outputs up to 100 psi (~ 6.8 bar).

2.3 Filter-candle tubing

Carbogen must be applied to the tissue through standard silicon cables via micro-filter candles. (e.g., models 18114 or 18124 in Robusglass, see this link). Be sure you have several meters of ROTILABO silicon tubes of different diameters (available, for example, here). Finally, you may also need tubing clamps to regulate the gas influx.

3. Solutions and drugs

It is critical to keep the cultures in BrainPhys Neuronal medium for at least three weeks before starting the recordings (see detailed formulation in Eichmüller et al., 2022). The standard culture in a high-nutrient formulation (e.g., Diff + Vitamin A from Lancaster et al., 2014) can be slowly replaced by increasing amounts of BrainPhys low-nutrient solution according to the following scheme:

Culture day Culture solution
Before day 40 100% high nutrient solution
day 40 80/20 ratio high/low nutrient solution
day 43 60/40 ratio high/low nutrient solution 
day 46 40/60 ratio high/low nutrient solution
day 49 20/80 ratio high/low nutrient solution
day 52 onwards 100% low nutrient solution

High nutrient formulation (Diff + Vitamin A)

This is the classic media for neuronal differentiation originally described for brain organoids (Lancaster et al., 2014). It is recommended to change the media every 2/3 days.

Low nutrient formulation (BrainPhys)

The low nutrient (~ 10 mM Glucose) formulation is based on Bardy et al., (2014) publication. Although it does not change drastically from the high-nutrient media described above, the proliferation and differentiation of neurons may be slightly reduced. Consequently, the size of the organoids in BrainPhys media is generally smaller when compared with the high nutrient formulation.
