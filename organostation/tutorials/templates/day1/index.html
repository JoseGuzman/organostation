1. Hardware requirements

Please, ask your building facility to assist you when installing the system for the first time. The equipment is heavy, but it is possible to move it with the help of a hand pallet trolley.

1.1 Dedicated location

You may need a quiet place for your station with the proper size. Depending on your model, you will have a 600 x 600 cm (Width x Length) for the recording system or a 600 x 900 cm passive isolator table for the recording workstation (see figure). Note that the table requires a surrounding distance of ~ 20 cm for electrical and mechanical isolation.

Computers, cell phones, or any other radio-emitting devices should not be exposed to the entry of the Faraday Cage, as they may interfere with the signals.

A pump will be used to pressurize the passive vibration table. We recommend filling the tables with 60 psi (415 kPa or 4.15 bars).

1.2 Power supply

It is recommended to use different security sockets for the recording system and the computer. If possible, two independent extra-long power strips are advisable

2.1 Carbogen bottle

A mixture of 5% CO2 and 95 % O2 (carbogen, available, for example, here) must be continuously supplied to the tissue to maintain the pH and appropriate oxygenation levels. Commercially available bottles containing 50L or 10 L of carbogen at 200 bars are available.

Carbogen is highly flammable. Check the safety regulations of your institution carefully.

2.2 Pressure adaptor

If your facility does not provide it, you will need an adaptor to reduce the pressure of the carbogen bottle (200 Bar) to the pressure of application necessary to operate the recording system (to ~ 1-2 bar pressure, 1 bar is 14.5 psi or 100 kPa). Please, also follow the local regulation (in Europe, we follow ISO 5171 regulations). Be sure that:

  1. The adaptor is suitable for conducting flammable gas.
  2. Input pressure is 200 bar ( in Europe DIN-477-1).
  3. Output pressure is in the range of 1-2 bars.
  4. The output size of the adaptor has a diameter of 6 or 8 mm.

You can order a suitable adaptor here that outputs up to 100 psi (~ 6.8 bar).

2.3 Filter-candle tubing

Carbogen must be applied to the tissue through standard silicon cables via micro-filter candles. (e.g., models 18114 or 18124 in Robusglass, see this link). Be sure you have several meters of ROTILABO silicon tubes of different diameters (available, for example, here). Finally, you may also need tubing clamps to regulate the gas influx.

3. Solutions and drugs

It is critical to keep the cultures in BrainPhys Neuronal medium for at least three weeks before starting the recordings (see detailed formulation in Eichmüller et al., 2022). The standard culture in a high-nutrient formulation (e.g., Diff + Vitamin A from Lancaster et al., 2014) can be slowly replaced by increasing amounts of BrainPhys low-nutrient solution according to the following scheme:

Culture day Culture solution
Before day 40 100% high nutrient solution
day 40 80/20 ratio high/low nutrient solution
day 43 60/40 ratio high/low nutrient solution 
day 46 40/60 ratio high/low nutrient solution
day 49 20/80 ratio high/low nutrient solution
day 52 onwards 100% low nutrient solution

3.1 Agarose 4% (w/v) in PBS

Prepare 4% (m/v) low-melt agarose (Biozime, Cat. No. 850080) in Phosphate Buffer Saline (PBS). We can store them in 1-2 mL stocks in Eppendorf tubes of 1.5 to 2 mL before warming them at ~ 40 degrees and embedding the tissue.
